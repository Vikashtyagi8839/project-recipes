Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food that consists of a spicy vegetable mash served with buttered bread rolls.

Ingredients

For Bhaji

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets, finely chopped
  • 1 cup boiled peas
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges

For Pav

  • 8 pav (bread rolls)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon pav bhaji masala (optional)

Instructions

To Make Bhaji

  1. Heat butter and oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped bell pepper, cauliflower, and boiled peas. Sauté for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and oil starts to separate from the masala.
  6. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes and mix well.
  7. Using a masher, mash all the vegetables together to a smooth consistency.
  8. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the bhaji and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Set aside.

To Toast Pav

  1. Slice the pav buns horizontally and apply butter on the cut sides.
  2. Sprinkle a little pav bhaji masala (optional) over the buttered sides.
  3. Heat a pan or tawa over medium heat and toast the pav buns, buttered side down, until golden and crisp.
  4. Remove from heat and set aside.

To Serve

  1. Serve the hot bhaji in a bowl, garnished with a dollop of butter, chopped coriander, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  2. Place the toasted pav buns on the side.
  3. Enjoy the bhaji by dipping the pav buns into it!

Notes:

  • You can adjust the spiciness of the bhaji by adding more or less red chili powder.
  • For extra flavor, add a little more butter to the bhaji before serving.
  • Use fresh pav buns for the best taste.
