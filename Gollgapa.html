Golgappa (Pani Puri)

A popular Indian street food, Golgappa or Pani Puri consists of crispy puris filled with spicy and tangy flavored water.

Ingredients

For Puri

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

For Spicy Water (Pani)

  • 1 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 4 cups cold water

For Filling

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup chickpeas, boiled
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

To Make Puri

  1. In a bowl, mix semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt.
  2. Add water little by little and knead into a firm dough.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into small circles.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the puris on medium heat until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  6. Remove and drain on paper towels. Let them cool completely before using.

To Make Spicy Water (Pani)

  1. In a blender, add mint leaves, coriander leaves, ginger, green chilies, and a little water. Blend to a smooth paste.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the paste with roasted cumin powder, black salt, chaat masala, salt, tamarind pulp, and 4 cups of cold water.
  3. Adjust the salt and spice levels as needed. Chill in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

To Make Filling

  1. In a bowl, mix mashed potatoes, chickpeas, chaat masala, and salt.
  2. Mix well and set aside.

To Assemble and Serve

  1. Gently make a hole in the center of each puri.
  2. Fill with a small amount of the potato-chickpea mixture.
  3. Dip the filled puri into the prepared spicy water and enjoy immediately!
